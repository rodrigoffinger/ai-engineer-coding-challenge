## 1. Purpose & Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) manual establishes the policies, procedures, and guidelines for the day-to-day operation of the grocery store. It is designed to ensure consistent, safe, and efficient operations across all departments while maintaining the highest standards of customer service and regulatory compliance.

### 1.2 Scope

This document applies to all employees, including full-time, part-time, temporary, and contract staff working in any capacity within the store. All personnel are expected to read, understand, and adhere to these procedures.

## 2. Store Hours & Opening Procedures

### 2.1 Standard Store Hours

| Day | Open | Close |
|-----|------|-------|
| Monday – Friday | 6:00 AM | 11:00 PM |
| Saturday | 7:00 AM | 11:00 PM |
| Sunday | 7:00 AM | 10:00 PM |
| Major Holidays | As posted | As posted |

### 2.2 Opening Checklist (Manager on Duty)

**Arrival — 45 Minutes Before Opening**

- [ ] Arrive at store, disarm security alarm
- [ ] Conduct a walk-through of the entire store
- [ ] Check all exterior lighting and parking lot conditions
- [ ] Inspect restrooms — restock paper products, clean if necessary
- [ ] Verify overnight stocking and receiving work is complete
- [ ] Confirm refrigeration units are at proper temperatures (log readings)
- [ ] Ensure all signage is current (prices, promotions, weekly ad)
- [ ] Check that all registers have adequate change funds
- [ ] Review previous day's incident reports and notes from closing manager
- [ ] Confirm staffing — contact replacements for any call-outs immediately
- [ ] Unlock all department storage areas
- [ ] Activate POS systems and verify network connectivity
- [ ] Conduct safety sweep: clear all emergency exits, check fire extinguishers
- [ ] Activate customer-facing lighting, music, and announcement systems

**At Opening Time**

- [ ] Unlock main entrance doors
- [ ] Confirm all scheduled staff are at their posts
- [ ] Make opening store announcement if applicable
- [ ] Log opening time and conditions in the Manager's Daily Log

## 3. Closing Procedures

### 3.1 Closing Checklist (Manager on Duty)

**Two Hours Before Close**

- [ ] Review sales performance vs. daily goals
- [ ] Begin rotating and reducing perishable displays
- [ ] Notify department leads to begin end-of-day restocking
- [ ] Confirm that all vendor deliveries have been checked in and signed off

**30 Minutes Before Close**

- [ ] Announce store closing to customers over PA system
- [ ] Begin final stocking and facing of all aisles
- [ ] Prepare registers for closing (reduce to minimum operating float)
- [ ] Begin cleaning procedures in deli, bakery, and produce

**At Close**

- [ ] Escort remaining customers to checkout
- [ ] Lock entrance doors once all customers have exited
- [ ] Collect all daily cash deposits; complete cash reconciliation report
- [ ] Secure all safes and lock all cash drawers
- [ ] Conduct final walk-through of all departments
- [ ] Shut down non-essential equipment (bakery ovens, deli slicers, fryers)
- [ ] Verify all refrigeration units are operational and logged
- [ ] Set security alarm
- [ ] Lock all exterior doors; verify loading dock is secured
- [ ] Complete and file Manager's Daily Closing Report

## 4. Employee Standards & Conduct

### 4.1 Dress Code & Appearance

- All employees must wear the designated store uniform (name badge, apron, department-specific attire)
- Uniforms must be clean, pressed, and in good repair at all times
- Closed-toe, non-slip shoes are mandatory for all floor staff
- Hair must be neat; hair nets are required in deli, bakery, and prepared foods departments
- Jewelry must be minimal and must not pose a safety hazard
- Artificial nails are prohibited in food handling departments

### 4.2 Attendance & Punctuality

- Employees must clock in no earlier than 7 minutes before their scheduled shift
- Tardiness of more than 5 minutes must be reported to the department supervisor
- Absences must be communicated to the store at least 2 hours before shift start
- Three unexcused absences within a 90-day period may result in disciplinary action

### 4.3 Professional Conduct

- Employees must maintain a respectful, professional attitude toward customers and colleagues at all times
- Personal cell phone use is prohibited on the sales floor; phones must be stored in lockers or back-of-house during shifts
- Personal shopping is not permitted while on duty
- Consumption of store merchandise without payment is prohibited and constitutes theft
- Employees must not discuss internal personnel matters, pricing strategies, or security measures with customers

### 4.4 Break Policy

| Shift Length | Paid Breaks | Unpaid Meal Break |
|---|---|---|
| 4 hours or less | None | None |
| 4–6 hours | 1 × 10 min | None |
| 6–8 hours | 1 × 10 min | 1 × 30 min |
| 8+ hours | 2 × 10 min | 1 × 30 min |

Breaks are to be taken in designated break rooms. Employees must not take breaks on the sales floor or in stockrooms visible to customers.

## 5. Customer Service Standards

### 5.1 The 10-Foot Rule

All employees must acknowledge any customer within 10 feet with eye contact, a smile, and a verbal greeting. Acceptable greetings include:
- "Good morning/afternoon/evening, welcome!"
- "Hi there! Is there anything I can help you find today?"

### 5.2 Handling Customer Inquiries

- Product location questions must be answered by walking the customer to the item, not simply pointing
- If an employee cannot answer a question, they must find someone who can — never leave a customer without assistance
- Product availability inquiries require checking the back stock before confirming an item is out of stock

### 5.3 Handling Complaints

**Steps for Complaint Resolution:**

1. Listen actively — do not interrupt the customer
2. Empathize sincerely ("I understand how frustrating that must be")
3. Apologize on behalf of the store
4. Offer a resolution (refund, exchange, replacement, or discount)
5. Escalate to a manager if the situation is not resolved within 2–3 minutes
6. Document all significant complaints in the Customer Complaint Log

**Empowerment Guidelines:** Front-line employees may issue refunds or replacements up to **$25.00** without manager approval.

### 5.4 Return & Refund Policy

| Item Type | Return Window | Condition Required | Proof of Purchase |
|---|---|---|---|
| Non-perishables | 30 days | Original packaging | Receipt preferred |
| Perishables | 7 days | Unopened or defective | Receipt required |
| Alcohol | No returns | — | — |
| Baby formula | 60 days | Unopened | Receipt required |
| Hot/prepared food | Same day only | Unsatisfactory quality | Not required |

## 6. Cash Handling & Point of Sale (POS)

### 6.1 Register Assignment

- Each cashier is assigned a unique login ID and is solely responsible for their register drawer
- Drawers must never be shared between cashiers during a shift without manager authorization
- Cashiers must count their drawer at the start and end of each shift and sign the Cash Count Sheet

### 6.2 Standard Cash Drawer Float

- Standard opening float: **$200.00**
- Bill denominations: 10 × $1, 4 × $5, 4 × $10, 2 × $20
- Coin rolls: 2 × pennies, 2 × nickels, 4 × dimes, 4 × quarters

### 6.3 Transaction Procedures

- All transactions must be completed through the POS system — no off-system sales
- Cashiers must announce the total aloud, state the amount tendered, and count change back to the customer
- $50 and $100 bills must be verified using a counterfeit detection pen before accepting
- Checks require a valid government-issued photo ID; ID number must be written on the check

### 6.4 Voids & Refunds

- Voids under $10: cashier may process with supervisor code
- Voids $10–$50: requires department supervisor approval and signature
- Voids over $50: requires Manager on Duty approval and written documentation

### 6.5 Cash Drop Procedures

- Cashiers must initiate a cash drop when their drawer exceeds **$500 in bills**
- Cash drops must be witnessed by the Manager on Duty
- All drops must be logged on the Cash Drop Log with time, amount, and signatures of both parties
- Cash is transported to the safe in sealed deposit bags — never carried openly on the sales floor

### 6.6 End-of-Day Reconciliation

1. Count all drawers with Manager on Duty present
2. Complete the Daily Sales Reconciliation Report
3. Acceptable variance: ±$2.00 per drawer
4. Variances exceeding ±$10.00 require a written Variance Report
5. Deposit all funds exceeding float into the store safe for bank pickup

## 7. Inventory Management

### 7.1 Inventory Cycle

| Cycle | Frequency | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| Full physical inventory | Quarterly | Store Manager + Ops Team |
| Department spot checks | Weekly | Department Lead |
| High-shrink item counts | Daily | Loss Prevention / Manager |
| Perpetual inventory updates | Continuous | POS System (automated) |

### 7.2 Ordering Procedures

- Department leads are responsible for submitting orders through the approved inventory management system
- Standard order lead time: 24–72 hours for primary supplier; 3–7 days for specialty suppliers
- Emergency orders must be approved by the Store Manager
- All orders must include: item number, quantity, unit of measure, and required delivery date

### 7.3 Minimum Stock Levels (Par Levels)

- Each department maintains a par level sheet for all core SKUs
- When on-hand quantity drops below par, a replenishment order is triggered automatically or manually initiated
- Par levels are reviewed and updated quarterly based on sales velocity data

### 7.4 Markdown & Clearance Procedures

- Products approaching expiration (within 3 days for perishables) must be marked down per the Markdown Schedule
- Markdown authority: Department Lead (up to 30%), Manager on Duty (up to 50%), Store Manager (over 50%)
- All markdowns must be processed through the POS system using the appropriate markdown code
- Items past expiration must be pulled immediately and logged in the Waste/Shrink Log

## 8. Receiving & Stocking

### 8.1 Receiving Dock Hours

- Standard receiving hours: **6:00 AM – 2:00 PM**, Monday through Saturday
- After-hours deliveries require prior written approval from the Store Manager

### 8.2 Receiving Checklist

- [ ] Verify driver ID and delivery manifest before unloading
- [ ] Inspect all outer cartons for damage before signing the delivery receipt
- [ ] Check product quantities against the purchase order
- [ ] Check all expiration dates — reject items with insufficient shelf life (see department minimums below)
- [ ] Verify cold chain: refrigerated items must arrive at ≤40°F; frozen at ≤0°F
- [ ] Log temperature of all perishable deliveries on the Receiving Temperature Log
- [ ] Sign delivery receipt only after full inspection — note any discrepancies in writing on the receipt
- [ ] Report all shortages and damages to the purchasing department within 24 hours

### 8.3 Minimum Acceptable Shelf Life at Receiving

| Category | Minimum Remaining Shelf Life |
|---|---|
| Dairy | 10 days |
| Meat & Poultry | 5 days |
| Deli meats (pre-packaged) | 7 days |
| Bread & Bakery | 3 days |
| Produce | 5 days |
| Canned / dry goods | 6 months |
| Frozen | 60 days |

### 8.4 Stocking Standards

- All products must be stocked using the **FIFO (First In, First Out)** method — new stock goes behind existing product
- Shelf labels must be checked and updated when restocking
- No product may be placed on shelves without a current, accurate price label
- Shelf integrity: all facings must be pulled to the front of the shelf (faced) before the store opens and after every major restocking
- Heavy items must be stocked on lower shelves; lighter items on upper shelves
- Maximum stack height for floor displays is **5 feet**

## 9. Food Safety & Handling

### 9.1 Core Food Safety Principles

All food handlers must adhere to the four core principles of food safety:

1. **Clean** — Wash hands frequently and properly; sanitize all surfaces and equipment
2. **Separate** — Keep raw meats away from ready-to-eat foods; use color-coded cutting boards
3. **Cook** — Ensure all prepared foods reach required internal temperatures
4. **Chill** — Refrigerate perishables promptly; never leave perishables in the temperature danger zone

### 9.2 Temperature Danger Zone

- **Danger Zone: 41°F – 135°F (5°C – 57°C)**
- Perishable foods must not remain in the danger zone for more than **2 hours cumulative**
- Foods held above 41°F for more than 4 hours must be discarded
- Temperature logs must be completed every **2 hours** for all display cases and walk-in coolers

### 9.3 Required Internal Cooking Temperatures

| Food Type | Minimum Internal Temperature |
|---|---|
| Poultry (all) | 165°F (74°C) |
| Ground beef / pork | 155°F (68°C) |
| Whole cuts of beef, pork, lamb | 145°F (63°C) |
| Fish and shellfish | 145°F (63°C) |
| Eggs (for immediate service) | 145°F (63°C) |
| Reheated foods | 165°F (74°C) |

### 9.4 Handwashing Requirements

Employees must wash hands:
- At the start of every shift
- After handling raw meat, poultry, or seafood
- After touching the face, hair, or body
- After using the restroom
- After handling garbage or chemicals
- After eating, drinking, or smoking
- After any potential contamination event

**Proper handwashing technique:** Wet hands → apply soap → scrub for **20 seconds** (back of hands, between fingers, under nails) → rinse thoroughly → dry with single-use paper towel → use paper towel to turn off faucet.

### 9.5 Personal Illness Policy

Employees must report to their supervisor and must not handle food if they are experiencing:
- Vomiting or diarrhea
- Jaundice (yellowing of skin or eyes)
- Sore throat with fever
- Infected wounds or lesions on hands

Employees diagnosed with Salmonella Typhi, Shigella, E. coli O157:H7, Hepatitis A, or Norovirus must not return to food handling until cleared by a licensed physician.

## 10. Perishable Department Procedures

### 10.1 Meat & Seafood Department

**Daily Opening Tasks**
- Verify cooler temperatures are at or below 38°F
- Inspect all displayed product — pull and discard any discolored or off-odor items
- Restock display cases using FIFO; rotate older product to the front
- Verify all price labels are accurate and legible
- Log opening temperatures

**Packaging Standards**
- All packaged meats must display: item name, weight, price per pound, total price, and sell-by date
- Packaging must be sealed airtight with no leaks or tears
- Ground beef must be packaged and labeled with the grind date; shelf life is 2 days from grind date

**Cutting Room Standards**
- All cutting boards must be sanitized between species (beef, pork, poultry, seafood)
- Color-coded cutting board system must be followed: Red = beef, Yellow = poultry, Blue = seafood, White = ready-to-eat
- Cutting equipment must be cleaned and sanitized every 4 hours and at end of shift

### 10.2 Dairy & Frozen Foods

- Cooler doors must remain closed unless actively stocking or retrieving product
- Frozen cases must maintain ≤0°F at all times
- Dairy cases must maintain 34°F–38°F
- Product must not be stocked above the load line in open cooler cases
- Defrost cycles must be logged and verified daily

## 11. Produce Department Procedures

### 11.1 Daily Receiving & Inspection

- Inspect all incoming produce for bruising, mold, insect damage, or temperature abuse
- Reject any items that do not meet quality standards and document on the Rejection Log
- Unpack and inspect all cases; never display produce in shipping boxes without approval

### 11.2 Display & Merchandising Standards

- Produce displays must be clean, well-organized, and visually appealing at all times
- Trim and rotate displays at minimum every **4 hours**
- Misting systems for leafy greens and herbs must be active during store hours
- Bulk bins must be cleaned and restocked daily; scoops must be sanitized every 2 hours

### 11.3 Shrink Management

- Produce that is no longer saleable at full price must be evaluated:
  - **Minor cosmetic damage:** Move to markdown/value section
  - **Suitable for prep:** Transfer to deli/prepared foods with appropriate documentation
  - **Unsaleable:** Log as waste in the Shrink Report and discard properly
- Daily shrink must be logged and reviewed weekly by the department lead

## 12. Deli & Prepared Foods

### 12.1 Food Production Standards

- All recipes must follow approved standardized recipe cards — no improvisation on ingredients or procedures
- Prepared items must be labeled with: item name, date and time prepared, use-by date/time, and preparer's initials
- Use-by times:
  - Hot held items: 4 hours from preparation
  - Cold prepared items: 7 days if sealed; 3 days if open
  - Sliced deli meats: 3 days from slicing

### 12.2 Slicer Operation & Safety

- Only trained and authorized employees may operate the meat slicer
- The blade guard must be in place at all times except during active slicing
- Slicer must be cleaned and sanitized:
  - At minimum every 4 hours during continuous use
  - Immediately after slicing raw meats before switching to ready-to-eat items
  - At end of shift (full breakdown and sanitization)
- Never leave a running slicer unattended

### 12.3 Hot Case Management

- Hot foods must be maintained at **135°F or above**
- Temperature checks must be logged every **2 hours**
- Items falling below 135°F must be discarded if they cannot be immediately reheated to 165°F
- Hot case must be drained, cleaned, and sanitized daily

### 12.4 Customer-Facing Service Standards

- Deli staff must wear gloves when handling all ready-to-eat foods
- Gloves must be changed: between handling different food types, after touching non-food surfaces, and after any contamination
- Customers must not reach into display cases; staff must retrieve all items

## 13. Bakery Department

### 13.1 Baking Operations

- All baking equipment must be preheated and verified at correct temperatures before use
- Bakers must verify ingredient freshness before use — do not use ingredients past their best-by date
- All baked goods must be cooled to room temperature before packaging to prevent condensation and mold
- Decoration and icing stations must be sanitized at the start of each shift

### 13.2 Custom Orders

- Custom cake and bakery orders must be recorded in the Order Book with: customer name, contact number, pickup date/time, item description, and price
- Orders require a minimum of **48 hours** notice for standard items; **5 business days** for specialty/wedding cakes
- A deposit of 50% is required for orders over $50
- Staff must confirm all custom orders by phone the day before pickup

### 13.3 Labeling & Allergen Compliance

- All packaged bakery items must clearly display: product name, ingredient list, net weight, price, and allergen statement
- The "Big 9" allergens must be identified on all product labels: milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soybeans, sesame
- Products containing allergens must be produced separately from allergen-free items, using dedicated equipment and utensils

## 14. Health & Safety Compliance

### 14.1 OSHA Compliance

- All employees must complete OSHA safety orientation within the first week of employment
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for all chemicals used in the store must be accessible and current
- Employees must be trained on all chemicals they use and understand the SDS for each

### 14.2 Hazard Communication

- All chemical containers must be labeled with product name, hazard warnings, and safe handling instructions
- Chemicals must never be stored in food containers or near food products
- Dilution instructions for cleaning chemicals must be posted at each mixing station

### 14.3 Slip, Trip & Fall Prevention

- Spills must be cleaned up immediately upon discovery — place wet floor signs before cleaning begins
- Wet floor signs must remain in place until the floor is completely dry
- All aisles and emergency exits must remain clear of obstacles at all times
- Mats must be flat and secured; any curled or damaged mat must be replaced immediately
- Employees must report any flooring damage, uneven surfaces, or lighting failures immediately

### 14.4 Lifting & Ergonomics

- Proper lifting technique is mandatory: bend at the knees, keep back straight, hold load close to the body
- Maximum single-person lift: **50 lbs** — team lift or mechanical aid required above this threshold
- Pallet jacks and hand trucks must be used for moving heavy freight
- Repetitive motion injuries or strain must be reported to the manager immediately

### 14.5 Equipment Safety

- Only trained and authorized employees may operate powered equipment (pallet jacks, balers, slicers, ovens)
- Safety guards on all equipment must be in place before operation
- Damaged equipment must be tagged "Out of Service" and reported for repair immediately — do not use damaged equipment
- Equipment maintenance and inspection logs must be completed per the schedule posted on each machine

## 15. Loss Prevention

### 15.1 Shoplifting Prevention

- Employees must greet all customers as they enter — this alone deters a significant percentage of theft
- High-theft items (razor blades, spirits, specialty meats, health/beauty) must be secured or monitored closely
- If suspicious behavior is observed, employees must:
  1. Alert the Manager on Duty immediately
  2. Continue to observe without approaching or accusing the individual
  3. Document the incident

> ⚠️ **Never physically confront or restrain a suspected shoplifter. This policy is non-negotiable and exists for the safety of all employees.**

### 15.2 Employee Theft Prevention

- All employee purchases must be made through a staffed register — self-checkout is prohibited for employees while on duty
- Employee bags and personal items must be stored in designated lockers; large bags are not permitted on the sales floor
- Sweethearting (failing to scan items or applying unauthorized discounts for a customer) will result in immediate termination
- Surveillance footage is monitored regularly; all discrepancies are investigated

### 15.3 Vendor Theft Prevention

- All vendor deliveries must be checked in by a store employee — never leave a vendor unattended in the stockroom
- Vendors are not permitted to stock their own product without a store employee present
- All credits, returns, and pull-outs by vendors must be documented and signed off by a manager

### 15.4 Shrink Reporting

- All known shrink (waste, spoilage, damage) must be logged in the Shrink Management System
- Department shrink reports are reviewed weekly
- Stores are targeted for a shrink rate below **2% of gross sales**

## 17. Emergency Procedures

### 17.1 Fire Emergency

1. Upon discovery of fire or alarm, immediately activate the nearest fire pull station
2. Call 911 immediately
3. Announce evacuation over the PA system: *"Attention shoppers and staff, please proceed calmly to the nearest exit."*
4. Direct all customers and staff to the designated assembly area (posted on building evacuation maps)
5. Do not attempt to fight a fire unless trained and the fire is very small and contained
6. Do not re-enter the building until authorized by the fire department
7. Manager on Duty accounts for all staff at the assembly area

### 17.2 Medical Emergency

1. Call 911 immediately for any serious injury or medical event
2. Do not move an injured person unless they are in immediate danger
3. Apply first aid only if trained to do so
4. Designate an employee to meet emergency responders at the entrance and guide them to the scene
5. Clear the area of non-essential personnel and customers
6. Complete an Incident Report within 24 hours

### 17.3 Robbery / Active Threat

**Robbery:**
1. Comply with all demands — no amount of money is worth a life
2. Do not attempt to resist, activate alarms, or use weapons
3. Activate the silent alarm only if it is safe to do so without the suspect noticing
4. Observe and remember the suspect's appearance (clothing, height, hair, distinguishing features)
5. After the suspect leaves, lock the doors and call 911 immediately
6. Preserve the scene — do not touch anything the suspect may have touched

**Active Threat/Intruder:**
1. Follow the **Run, Hide, Fight** protocol:
   - **Run:** If there is a safe escape route, evacuate immediately
   - **Hide:** If escape is not possible, secure yourself in a locked room, silence phones, and stay quiet
   - **Fight:** As a last resort, use any available items to defend yourself
2. Call 911 when safe to do so
3. When law enforcement arrives: show hands, follow all instructions, do not make sudden movements

### 17.4 Power Outage

- Immediately check all refrigeration and freezer units with backup thermometers
- Refrigerated food is safe if the power outage is under **4 hours** and the units remain closed
- Frozen food is safe if it remains at or below **40°F**
- If power outage exceeds 4 hours, notify the Store Manager immediately for direction on product disposition
- Activate emergency lighting — verify all emergency exits are illuminated
- Do not open additional registers until POS systems are fully restored and verified

### 17.5 Severe Weather

- Monitor NOAA weather alerts during all high-risk weather events
- In the event of a tornado warning: direct all customers and staff to the interior of the store, away from windows and exterior walls
- Do not allow customers to leave during an active tornado warning
- All staff must know the location of the interior shelter area (posted on emergency maps)

## 18. Human Resources & Scheduling

### 18.1 Scheduling Policy

- Schedules are published no fewer than **7 days in advance**
- Shift swap requests must be submitted in writing to the supervisor at least **48 hours** in advance
- The store will make reasonable accommodation for religious observances with sufficient notice
- Overtime (over 40 hours/week) requires prior written approval from the Store Manager

### 18.2 Onboarding & Training

**New Employee Onboarding Checklist:**
- [ ] Complete all new hire paperwork (I-9, W-4, direct deposit)
- [ ] Review and sign Employee Handbook acknowledgment
- [ ] Complete store tour and safety orientation
- [ ] Complete food handler certification (within first 30 days if in food handling role)
- [ ] Complete register training (if applicable)
- [ ] Shadow a department trainer for minimum **3 shifts**
- [ ] Complete online OSHA safety training modules
- [ ] Receive and sign for uniform and equipment

### 18.3 Performance Management

- Performance reviews are conducted **semi-annually** (every 6 months)
- Disciplinary actions follow a progressive system:
  1. Verbal warning (documented)
  2. Written warning
  3. Final written warning / Suspension
  4. Termination
- Certain violations (theft, harassment, violence, food safety endangerment) may result in immediate termination

### 18.4 Harassment & Discrimination Policy

The store maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment or discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, sexual orientation, or any other protected class. All complaints must be reported to HR or the Store Manager and will be investigated promptly and confidentially.

- New vendors must complete the vendor application process and be approved by corporate purchasing before making any deliveries

- Vendors must follow all store safety rules and may not interfere with store operations

## 20. Compliance & Regulatory Requirements

### 20.1 Food Safety Certifications

- At least **one certified Food Protection Manager (FPM)** must be on duty at all times during operational hours
- Food handler cards must be maintained and current for all food-handling employees
- Certifications must be kept on file and available for inspection at any time

### 20.2 Health Department Inspections

- All health department inspectors must be greeted professionally and given immediate, full cooperation
- The Store Manager or designee must accompany the inspector throughout the inspection
- Violations must be corrected within the time frame specified by the inspector
- All inspection reports must be retained on file for a minimum of **3 years**

### 20.3 Alcohol Sales Compliance

- Alcohol may only be sold to customers who can present valid, government-issued photo ID confirming they are 21 or older
- When in doubt, always card — employees may card any customer regardless of apparent age
- All employees selling alcohol must be trained in responsible alcohol sales (state-mandated certification may apply)
- Alcohol may not be sold to visibly intoxicated individuals
- Violation of alcohol compliance laws may result in immediate termination
